Scope & method— how churn is measured
Churn = accounts that received ≥1 payment in a prior month but none in the cycle. Lost Payments ARR = annualized payments value of those accounts at their last-active run-rate. Counts accounts that received payments only (excludes never-paid).

Card share hit a 16-month low (17.69%) — and each 0.5pp of share ≈ $350K in ARR. The drop is entirely first-time-driven: the FT slide bleeds into returning a month later, so the blend keeps slipping even as May FT adoption rebounded +2.3pp (it reaches returning in June).
Why first-time card share bounced — “Costello” = pure-ACH onboarding. Costello Management, a large PM, onboards first-time tenants at 0% card / 100% ACH. The batch started Feb ’26 (a tiny ~100%-card account before), surged to ~700 first-timers in March (~$1.1M, all ACH), tailed through April (228), then rolled off in May (56). ACH-only batches mechanically drag first-time card-share down — the May rolloff drove ~¼–½ of the FT rebound.

Card performance · the why

The card drop was a Spring-’25 step — then a slow grind to new lows.

Driver 1 · rising rent
Card share by payment amount
Card share collapses 54%→10% as the amount climbs. Median first-time rent rose $1,050→$1,150 (+10%) over Jan ’25→May ’26, so every cohort drifts down mechanically.
Driver 2 · ACH-default onboarding batches
First-time card adoption · with vs without ~0%-card batches · Feb '25 → May '26
Recurring ACH-default onboarding batches (e.g. Costello) drag FT card adoption — modest most months, spiking to −2.8pp when a large batch lands (Mar ’26). Net of batches the line still erodes (rent = Driver 1); batches are the swing — lever: default new-PM onboarding to card.
Two first-time drivers: rent rising mechanically pushes card down (54%→10% as the amount climbs), and recurring ACH-default onboarding batches add a lumpy ~0.5–2.8pp drag.
✓ Ruled out — existing-tenant switching: card stickiness rose 91.3% → 92.8% (existing payers more loyal, not less). ✓ Ruled out — a PM-tier mix shift: first-timers didn’t move to lower-card tiers (Emerging+SMB held ~36% of FT, flat YoY); the drop is a within-tier rate decline (SMB −3pp, Mid-Market −2pp), not low-tier dilution. The decline lives entirely on the FT side.

Methodology— cohort, eligibility, method & caveats

Cohort: customers whose first-EVER payment landed in cohort_cycle (payments_month basis — 25-of-prior → 24-of-this, matches the rest of the deck). Window: Sep '25 → May '26 (mirrors slide 7's cohort window). Lease-frequency filter: Weekly / Daily / Monthly / Every2Weeks.

Eligibility-adjusted denominator: % = paid / eligible, where eligible = the cohort-defining lease has status='ACTIVE' on STG_LEASE_DAILY during target_cycle. This strips out forced churn (lease ended, eviction, PM gone) — what's left measures voluntary payment behavior. When the lease is ACTIVE its dbtenant is by definition still on platform, so the dbtenant-active condition is implied.

Method filters: Card / ACH views restrict the cohort to customers whose first-ever payment was by that method. The Retention toggle controls the numerator — same-method-only vs any subsequent payment (the gap = switchers).

Caveats: Card and ACH first-method cohorts can overlap (~3%) when a customer paid with both methods in their first month. Trailing nulls = cohort+offset past May '26 (out of window). M0 ≈ 100% by construction.
