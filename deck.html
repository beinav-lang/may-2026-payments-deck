09
Card Performance · The Why

The card drop wasn't gradual — it was a Spring 2025 step-down. Both cohorts. Same months.

The card decline isn’t gradual — it’s a Spring-’25 step-down hitting both cohorts at once. Drivers: rising rent (card share collapses 54%→10% as amount climbs) plus a one-time FT-adoption step; switching is ruled out. And it now compounds: card M1 retention itself stepped down ~3pp in 2026 (62.5% Jan → ~59% Feb–Apr), so each FT cohort graduates into returning holding card at a lower rate.

Mar–Aug '25 both cohorts crashed in lockstep: Onboarding 50% → 41%, Mature 54% → 46% (~8–10pp each, in 4–5 months). Since Aug '25 they've bounced in a low band with no clear trend. Mix neutral, switching ruled out, amount <1pp. Two fingerprints point the same way: the drop is segment-agnostic (every PM tier fell 8–11pp from the Mar '25 peak), and PMs onboarding after spring '25 start ~6pp lower at identical rent.

▶ Methodology · cohort definition + caveats
Cohort: customers whose first-EVER payment landed in cohort_cycle (payments_month basis — 25-of-prior → 24-of-this, matches the rest of the deck). Window: Sep '25 → May '26 (mirrors slide 7's cohort window). Lease-frequency filter: Weekly/Daily/Monthly/Every2Weeks.
Eligibility-adjusted denominator: % = paid / eligible, where eligible = the cohort-defining lease has status='ACTIVE' on STG_LEASE_DAILY during target_cycle. This strips out forced churn (lease ended, eviction, PM gone) — what's left measures voluntary payment behavior. When the lease is ACTIVE its dbtenant is by definition still on platform, so the dbtenant-active condition is implied.
Method filters: Card/ACH views restrict the cohort to customers whose first-ever payment was by that method. The Retention toggle controls the numerator — same-method-only vs any subsequent payment (the gap = switchers).
Caveats: Card and ACH first-method cohorts can overlap (~3%) when a customer paid with both methods in their first month. Trailing nulls = cohort+offset past May '26 (out of window). M0 ≈ 100% by construction.

Why the funnel uses a 45-day window?

Methodology Cohort & exclusion ▾
Cohort = leases first-active in the selected month, on a PM with active Merchant Account and a rent frequency of Monthly or shorter. Mature = lease activated ≥90 days after the PM's first invoice; Onboarding = activated within 90 days. Re-tenant exclusion (applied to all months & all stages): drop a lease if any of its tenants has invitationAcceptedAt before the lease activation AND appears on ≥1 other lease (~9% of Mature each month). All numbers below use this Ex-re-tenants cohort. Stage sources: Invited and Invite page-view are PostHog product events (invitation_sent / invitation_pageview), matched to the lease by the invite recipient (sentToId) within ±45 days of activation; Registered = tenant portalInfo.invitationAcceptedAt (±45d); Converted/Retained from Stripe payments.
