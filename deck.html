Executive Summary

May gave back April's gain — paid % fell 1.18pp to 46.88%: active jumped +10K but paid count barely moved (+252). The PM-side engine quietly hit a new high.

TL;DR

Verdict: May regressed to 46.88% paid (−1.18pp MoM) from April's 48.06% record. Paid leases held nearly flat at 181.9K (+252) while active grew +10.1K (+2.7%) — the denominator moved, the numerator didn't. Underneath: reactivations dropped −27% MoM (9.5K → 6.9K), headline churned leases at 21.0K (+19% MoM) — but ~half of "tenant churn" is partial-cycle artifact (rent paid May 25–31 lands in next cycle bucket); projected true May churn ≈14.2K, down from Apr's 17.7K (slide 7). UpMarket paid count actually fell −701 (paid % −3.42pp) — segments softened on conversion too. PM-side bright spot: Mature invite-at-activation hit 74.07% (+14.6pp MoM, new record) — the leading PM indicator improved sharply even as tenant-side conversion regressed. Also in the back half: PM-cancel churn +35% MoM, +227% YoY (small absolute count but the highest-growth churn category); PM Adoption analysis identifies ~23% of accounts flaggable with a 0.94-AUC risk score; Q2 lever portfolio still ~$675K ARR + 4pp funnel uplift if all four land. Data caveat: May is a partial snapshot (through May 24). ARR vs plan, card share, NSF, and churned-account metrics use the most recent fully-settled month (April) — see slide footers.

Risk score — why and how turning the levers + leading indicators into one CSM-ready signal ▾
Why. The matrix above shows adoption is sticky — half of Lows at day 90 stay stuck. By the time outcome data is conclusive, the window to intervene has already closed. We need to detect risk earlier and direct CSM action to the specific lever that's broken, not "this account is in trouble" generically.
Day 1 — Setup audit
Settings observable from the moment onboarding ends
  • Online payments fully enabled (lever #1)
  • ACH / CC not blocked in tenant portal
  • Rent reminders turned on (lever #5)
  • Bank Connect + receive-payments feature on
Day 30 — Process snapshot
First behavioral signals

  • First invite events sent — 43% of Lows sent zero invites in their first 6 months (lever #2)

Patterns starting to crystallize

First closed-data read
  • % paid leases this month — the matrix predictor
  • Tier @ day 90 determines stickiness — 50% of 0–20% stay stuck
AUC 0.9412-flag risk score
How: each leading indicator becomes a 1/0 flag with weights from a logistic regression (target = "is Low adopter"). Sum to a single 0–25 score. Score ≥ 7 catches 23% of the cohort and is dominated by future Lows (49% Low / 14% High).
→
Delivery (pipeline). Daily snapshot of (a) account settings, (b) leases / invite events / payments, (c) SF engagement timestamps. Score + per-flag detail surface as a Salesforce field on each account, plus a CSM dashboard view sorted by score.
Methodology How the score is calculated ▾
1. Define 12 binary flags per account — each fires (= 1) if the bad condition is true.
2. Train a logistic regression with the 12 flags as predictors and "is this account a future Low adopter?" as target. The model learns each flag's marginal predictive weight.
3. Convert coefficients to integer points (round of LR coef × 2). The scorecard:

4. Risk score = sum of points across all firing flags. Range in practice: 0–25.
Example: a PM with no merchant account (+8), online payments not engaged (+7), and slow invite lag (+3) scores 18 — very high risk. A PM with only no-bank-connect firing scores 1 — clean.
* These three are real signals univariately (lift 2.5–5×) but collinear with the higher-weighted flags. The regression assigns them no marginal weight; keep them as secondary warnings in the dashboard. AUC = 0.94 in-sample (target: predict Low adopter); out-of-time validation is a step on the v2 roadmap.

So what (next moves). Three things, in order: (1) Pilot now — the ~2,150 accounts already at score ≥ 7 are a ready-made CSM list; the action follows the flag (e.g. "ACH disabled" → settings fix; "no first payment by day 30" → onboarding call). (2) Productize the score as a SF field, refreshed at day-30 / 60 / 90. (3) Validate with a controlled rollout — measure lift on the at-risk cohort vs. a hold-out so we know intervention is causal, not just correlated.
